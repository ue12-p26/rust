## CEA

:height: 120px

The French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission
([Commissariat à l'énergie atomique et aux énergies alternatives](https://www.cea.fr/))
is a French public government-funded research organisation, with a permanent
staff of 21,000 persons.

The CEA is involved in multiple
[research areas](https://www.cea.fr/english/Pages/research-areas.aspx)
and is spread across several centers (see {numref}`fig-centers`):

- Defence and security.
- Nuclear energy.
- Renewable energies.
- Technological research for industry.
- Health and life sciences.
- Matter and universe.
- Climate and environment.

## CNRGH

:::{image} media/cnrgh_logo.png
:alt: CNRGH logo
:align: right
:height: 120px
:::

The French National Center of Human Genomics Research
([Centre National de Recherche en Génomique Humaine](https://jacob.cea.fr/drf/ifrancoisjacob/english/Pages/Departments/CNRGH.aspx))
is part of the CEA Paris-Saclay center.

It is one of the departments of the IBFJ
([Institut de Biologie François Jacob](https://www.cea.fr/drf/ifrancoisjacob/english)):

- **CNRGH**: research in human genomics.
- **Genoscope**: environmental genomics and the exploration of metabolic
  functions.
- **IRCM**: radiobiology and radiotoxicology.
- **IDMIT**: prevention and treatment of infectious diseases.
- **MIRCen**: diagnosis and treatment of neurodegenerative diseases.

In addition, the Institute hosts two services involved in research and
technological innovations regarding prion diseases (SEPIA), and
immunohematology (SRHI).

The laboratories of the CNRGH:

- Biobank (LB).
- Bioanalysis (LBA).
- Bioinformatics (LBI).
- Development (LD).
- Epigenetic and Environment (LEE).
- Functional Genomics (LFG).
- Production Platforms in Human Genomics (L2PGH).
